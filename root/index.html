Algebra Phase 1 Dashboard (Weeks 1–3)

Use this as a checklist for your first 3 weeks of algebra review before the Calculus Review for Data Science course. Each "Session" is one 1‑hour study block.

How to use this: Treat each session as one evening. Check tasks off as you complete them. If you prefer specific days, write them next to the session titles or print this page and mark it up.

Week 1 – Foundations & Linear Equations Core algebra

Focus on order of operations, simplifying expressions, and solving 1–2 step equations.

Session 1 – Warm‑up & order of operations
  • Watch: Algebra For Beginners – Basic Introduction (first ~25 minutes).
  • Khan Academy: In Algebra foundations, do the practice sets for order of operations & combining like terms.
  • Do at least 10 mixed problems involving parentheses, exponents, and fractions (from Khan practice or a textbook section).

Links
Khan Academy – Algebra foundations: Open unit

Session 2 – Solving linear equations
  • Review 1‑step and 2‑step equations videos in the Algebra foundations / Algebra 1 course.
  • Complete at least 2 sets of Khan practice on solving linear equations (including equations with fractions).
  • Write 5 of your own word problems that translate to linear equations, and solve them.

Links
Khan Academy – Algebra 1 main page: Open course

Session 3 – Inequalities & absolute value
  • Khan Academy: Watch/complete intro lessons on inequalities and absolute value equations/inequalities.
  • Do 10–15 inequality problems (including graphing on a number line) and check your answers with the solution key or Khan feedback.
  • Summarize on paper: rules for adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing inequalities (including when the sign flips).

Week 2 – Exponents & Expressions Fluency with symbols

Build confidence with exponent rules and simplifying variable expressions.

Session 4 – Exponent rules
  • Watch: continue or rewatch the exponent section of Algebra For Beginners focusing on exponent laws.
  • Khan Academy: Complete practice on properties of exponents (product, quotient, power rules, zero and negative exponents).
  • Do a 10‑problem "quiz" you create yourself: simplify 10 expressions with exponents, then check using Khan or a calculator.
Session 5 – Simplifying expressions
  • Khan Academy: Work through simplifying algebraic expressions (distribute, combine like terms).
  • From an algebra text (e.g., OpenStax Intermediate/College Algebra), do ~10 problems on simplifying expressions and check against the answer key.
  • Add the main exponent and simplification rules to your one‑page "cheat sheet".

Links
OpenStax – College Algebra 2e answers (example chapter): Answer key sample

Session 6 – Multiplying polynomials
  • Watch Khan or a short YouTube clip on multiplying binomials (FOIL) and distributing across parentheses.
  • Khan Academy: Complete the multiplying polynomials practice set.
  • Do 5–10 textbook problems on multiplying binomials/trinomials and verify with the answer key.

Week 3 – Factoring & Rational Expressions Pre‑calculus prep

Prepare for limits by getting comfortable factoring and simplifying expressions like (x²−1)/(x−1).

Session 7 – Factoring quadratics (easy cases)
  • Khan Academy: Watch the intro videos on factoring quadratics where the leading coefficient is 1.
  • Do 10–15 factoring problems of the form x² + bx + c and check answers via Khan or answer key.
  • Write down a 3‑step checklist you follow when you factor a quadratic.
Session 8 – Factoring with a≠1 and special forms
  • Khan Academy: Practice factoring quadratics where the leading coefficient is not 1, and special patterns (difference of squares).
  • Do at least 10 mixed factoring problems from a textbook and verify odd‑numbered answers.
  • Add examples of each special pattern (e.g., (x+a)(x−a)) to your cheat sheet.
Session 9 – Rational expressions & checkpoint
  • Khan Academy: Work through simplifying rational expressions by factoring numerator/denominator and canceling common factors.
  • Do 8–10 rational expression problems from Khan or a textbook, focusing on ones that look like the kinds of expressions you might see in limits.
  • End‑of‑phase check: Take the Algebra 1 Course Challenge on Khan Academy as a diagnostic and note any weak areas for later review.
